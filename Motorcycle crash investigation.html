Riding Safe: Strategies to Minimize Motorcycle Crash Risks

Introduction

It’s widely understood that motorcycle riding carries a higher risk of injury compared to driving a car. What steps can motorcyclists take to lessen their chances of getting hurt? Our investigation focuses on the behaviors of road users to uncover possible safety measures.

Please use the table of contents on the left to navigate quickly.

Data Cleaning

Data is obtained from Data.NSW. There are two data sets. First is “crash_data”, which documents the result of the crash. Second is “traffic_unit_data, which records driving patterns just before the crash for each traffic unit involved.

We clean column names and inner join the two based on crash_id. We need the traffic_unit_data because it specifies whether a motorcycle is involved in a crash.

Code 
crash_data_raw = read_excel("data/nsw_road_crash_data_2016-2020_crash.xlsx")
traffic_unit_data_raw = read_excel("data/nsw_road_crash_data_2016-2020_traffic_unit.xlsx")

crash_data = crash_data_raw %>% clean_names()
traffic_unit_data = traffic_unit_data_raw %>% clean_names()
merged_dataset = merge(crash_data, traffic_unit_data, by = "crash_id")

Recommendations

A motorcyclist can do the following to reduce the risk of crash:

First, always lane filter. Lane filtering is legal in NSW if the traffic is slow-moving or stopped, and the motorcyclist is travelling below 30 km/h. Motorcyclist can significantly reduce the risk of being rear ended by a car by lane filtering to the top the queue where cars are stopped already. The legislation forbids lane filtering adjacent to parked vehicles or large trucks, adherence to which can minimize the likelihood of rollovers.

Second, if the opposing traffic is indicating to turn right or turning right, slow down. Data indicate that vehicles making right turns are frequently involved in severe injury incidents. When navigating an intersection with numerous parked cars blocking the view, it’s advisable to reduce speed to prevent unexpected encounters.

Third, do not ride with overconfidence. Most roll over crashes happened with loss of control. Weather and many other unpredictable factors can lead to roll overs.

Fourth, motorcycle hitting a road object causes many crashes. To prevent such incidents, riders should abstain from consuming alcohol and consider enhancing lighting or reducing speed during nighttime.
